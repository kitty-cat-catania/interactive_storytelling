U.S. Census Bureau, Current Population Survey, Annual Social and Economic Supplement 1999-2020

Although moving for reasons related to housing is the most common reason for moving throughout the past two decades, the four different reasons do begin to converge, but only slightly. There was a steep decline in moving for 'Other' during the period between 2010 and 2014. One could extrapolate that this is likely at least mildly correlated with the aftermath of the Great Recession of 2008, during which the housing market collapsed and many Americans struggled financially.

Total Movers Over Time

This line chart graphs total movers in America (domestic, movers from abroad, and Americans moving abroad) from 1948 until 2020.

Total movers peaks in 1985, then steadily declines. During recessionary periods, there is a sharp decline in total movers. This is evidenced by the steep downward trends in 2001 and in 2008. Also noteworthy is the possible correlation between the steep rise in total movers during the period of 1984-1995 and the economic expansion of those years.

The trend of decling total movers was surprising to us, as we hypothesized that the number of total movers would increase over time.
